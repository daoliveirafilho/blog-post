## Introduction

Secure your remote infrastructure with automated permissions and identity controls.

Privileged Access Management (PAM) is a core cybersecurity strategy that secures and monitors accounts with elevated permissions.

<InfoAlert title="Important Note">
It restricts "keys to the kingdom" access to minimize the damage from external hackers and malicious insiders.
</InfoAlert>

## Main Content

### The Principle of Least Privilege:

Users and processes are granted only the minimum access rights strictly necessary to perform their roles.

### Secure Vaulting:

Administrators do not directly view or memorize passwords. PAM platforms vault them securely and inject credentials directly into the session.

### Just-in-Time (JIT) Access:

Replaces permanent administrative rights with temporary access that is automatically revoked upon task completion.

### Session Monitoring & Auditing:

Records and logs all privileged actions, ensuring strict traceability for regulatory compliance.
